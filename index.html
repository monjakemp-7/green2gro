What Fresh Feed
Does for Your Animals

Green2Gro fodder chambers grow nutrient-dense sprouted barley, oats and wheatgrass in just 6 to 8 days, giving livestock a living, enzyme-rich feed that transforms animal health and production outcomes. The science is clear and the results speak for themselves.

"Feeding sprouted grain to livestock is not a new idea. European dairy farmers were doing it in the 1800s to maintain milk through winter. What Green2Gro has done is bring industrial precision and year-round consistency to a practice that has worked for centuries."

Backed by two decades of applied research from our founder, Nick Kemp, who has been developing germinated-grain feed systems since 2005 and was featured as an industry expert in Landbou.com's "Ask the Expert" column on the science of grain germination for livestock feed.

Aeroponic, Not HydroponicBasic hydroponic fodder trays sit in a shallow layer of standing water — the exact condition that causes mould risk and inconsistent results in older green-feed systems. Green2Gro chambers use true aeroponics instead: roots hang in open air and receive an ultra-fine, precisely timed mist, with no standing water at any point.

Enzyme-Rich Living FeedGermination converts barley, oats or wheatgrass seed into a far more digestible feed: digestibility rises from roughly 30% in dry seed to 90–95% once sprouted, protein content increases from around 8% to 21%, and biomass weight increases by up to 620% within 8 days.

Year-Round Supply, Any ClimateUnlike seasonal pasture, a Green2Gro fodder chamber delivers the same fresh, clean, pesticide-free feed every single day regardless of drought, frost or feed price spikes.

Engineered for Unit EconomicsBasic tray-based setups struggle with cost per kilogram at scale. Automated climate control, precision misting and multi-tier racking are what make consistent, commercial-scale unit economics achievable — not manual trays and guesswork.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

The questions we hear most often from farmers, agribusinesses and investors evaluating a CEA system.

Is Green2Gro's technology hydroponic or aeroponic?
Aeroponic. Roots hang in open air and are fed an ultra-fine, precisely timed nutrient mist — there is no standing water at any point, unlike basic hydroponic tray systems. This is what avoids the mould risk and inconsistent results associated with older hydroponic fodder setups.
How long does it take to grow fodder in a Green2Gro chamber?
Sprouted barley, oats or wheatgrass fodder is ready to feed to livestock in 6 to 8 days, with digestibility rising from roughly 30% in dry seed to 90–95% once sprouted.
How much water does a Green2Gro system save compared to conventional farming?
Up to 95% less water than conventional soil-based farming, with growing possible 365 days a year regardless of climate.
What can I grow in a Green2Gro CEA chamber?
Livestock fodder (wheatgrass, barley and oats) for dairy cattle, beef cattle, sheep, poultry, pigs and game, plus human-consumption crops including microgreens, exotic mushrooms, leafy greens, herbs, wheatgrass shots and sprouts.
What's the typical return on investment?
Clients typically see a return on their investment within 12 to 18 months of commissioning, driven by year-round growing, reduced input costs and premium crop quality.
Where does Green2Gro operate?
Green2Gro is based in South Africa with installations across South Africa, Namibia and Canada, and serves clients worldwide. Our systems are designed and tested in sub-Saharan conditions, so they handle heat, drought and power variability that would trip up most other solutions on the market.
Can emerging or land-reform farmers get funding for a Green2Gro system?
Possibly — infrastructure investments like CEA and fodder chambers can fall within the scope of South Africa's Comprehensive Agricultural Support Programme (CASP) grants for land reform beneficiaries and smallholder farmers. Get in touch and we can talk through funding options relevant to your situation.
